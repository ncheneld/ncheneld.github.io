Research

Initially, we created a business model looking at different aspects of our product including our value proposition, cost/revenue, customer relationships, barriers to entry as well as the business model of competing apps. We performed a top down and bottom up market analysis focused on targeting users in the US and then interviewed various groups of potential users, including parents (who would approve of purchases for the app), mobile users, casual gamers, and hardcore gamers. For each group, we looked at what they think about mobile games, their goals and motivations in terms of mobile apps, and what influences them to play or buy apps.


After an inital implementation of a basic app, we conducted a usability test. For each stage of gameplay, We would observe their actions and ask them to complete tasks. We looked at the time taken to complete/attempt tasks, whether or not they figured out how to complete actions, how many buttons or screen taps they performed before completing an action correctly, and whether they realized they had accomplished the task. Based on the results, we added more features, hints, and tips to make gameplay easier and smoother.
